Project Design for Project 3: Wine Quality Data


Overview

Let’s talk about wine quality! What makes a good wine? We know everyone has their own particular wine preferences, but are there any scientific measures that indicate a good or bad wine? If so, what are the max and min acceptable ranges for different measures to still have a good wine? Which measures are more important for the overall quality score versus the ones that don’t affect it too much? And what does the user’s preference for wine type say about their taste in wine quality?


Data

Our data measured a number of things for each wine:

  • Fixed acidity
  • Volatile acidity
  • Citric acid
  • Residual sugar
  • Chlorides
  • Free sulfur dioxide
  • Total sulfur dioxide
  • Density
  • pH
  • Sulphates
  • Alcohol
  • Output variable (based on sensory data): Quality Score (score between 0 and 10)

Each of these categories is a numberical value, which are then used to determine the Quality Score.


Data Sources

Our data comes from a University of Californa - Irvine study done on wine quality from 2009, source data and information can be found on the Resources page as well as in our "data" folder of the repo.
